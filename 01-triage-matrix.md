## Methodology Note

Each alert below was originally investigated in isolation by a different analyst on a different shift. This matrix re-evaluates each one **using only the evidence available at the time each ticket was closed**, then applies the **correlation lens** that was missing: does a common entity appear across multiple alerts, and does the combined sequence change the signal/noise call?

Severity re-scoring follows two tracks:
- **SIEM Severity** — the nominal classification generated by the detection rule.
- **Operational Severity** — the analyst's re-assignment based on context, correlation, and available evidence.

## Alert 1 — TKT-04102

**Rule Hypothesis:** Possible brute-force attack against `admin_backup`; account locked after threshold breach, followed by a successful post-unlock login.

**Evidence available:**
- Fri 14:25:11 — `LOCKOUT admin_backup` (15 attempts, threshold reached)
- Fri 14:48:03 — `LOGIN_SUCCESS admin_backup` from `10.50.8.45` (post-unlock)
- CHG-2026-1842 (prior Monday): quarterly password rotation for the environment — referenced in ticket, **not independently verified against ITSM records**
- Email activity: no mailbox enabled for `admin_backup`; no emails sent or received in Fri–Sat window — phishing as access vector ruled out
- Source IP `10.50.8.45` is an internal address — identity of the device and its owner not confirmed against asset inventory

**Signal / Noise call:** Noise as a standalone brute-force. A quarterly password rotation plausibly explains repeated failed logins by the legitimate account owner. The successful login from an internal IP does not, by itself, confirm unauthorized access. **The prior analyst's false-positive closure was defensible in isolation.**

**Why it stays in scope:** This is the session anchor. Every event in the subsequent alerts traces back to `admin_backup` credentials authenticated at Fri 14:48:03. Individually inconclusive; contextually, the entry point of the full chain.

## Alert 2 — TKT-04118

**Rule Hypothesis:** `admin_backup` accessed `\\srv-backup-01\Backup_Config\` outside of expected operational hours and read Active Directory hash data. Anomalous process lineage detected during the same session: `powershell.exe -EncodedCommand` executed with `rsync.exe` as the parent process.

**Evidence available:**
- Fri 14:48:03 — `LOGIN_SUCCESS admin_backup` from `10.50.8.45` (session start, continuous)
- Fri 14:50 — access to `Backup_Config` resource, including AD hashes *(INFERRED — not directly confirmed in a discrete log entry; derived from alert context)*
- Fri 22:12 — `powershell.exe -EncodedCommand` launched by `rsync.exe` (EDR)
- Session remained active from Fri 14:48 through at least Sat 06:00 — more than 15 hours without interruption
- SIEM reports exfiltration under 30 minutes; firewall shows multiple transfer windows across several hours — **clock/source discrepancy, unresolved** (see Timeline, `03-timeline.md`)

**Signal / Noise call:** Signal. `rsync.exe → powershell.exe -EncodedCommand` is not legitimate backup process behavior. An encoded PowerShell command with a backup utility as its parent process, immediately before an external connection is established, is an anomalous execution chain that does not have an innocent explanation supported by the available evidence.

**Why it matters beyond this ticket:** This event bridges the initial session (TKT-04102) and the exfiltration (TKT-04156). It establishes that something executed on `srv-backup-01` before the outbound transfer — whatever was encoded in that PowerShell command is unrecovered evidence.

| SIEM Severity | HIGH |
| Operational Severity | CRITICAL |
| Correlate with others? | Yes |
| Common anchor | Account: `admin_backup` · Host: `srv-backup-01` · Process: `powershell.exe -EncodedCommand` · Parent: `rsync.exe` · Time: Fri 22:12 |

## Alert 3 — TKT-04156

**Rule Hypothesis:** Outbound data transfer from `srv-backup-01` to an external IP with no organizational history. Transfer volume: ~50 GB over HTTPS/443. A previously unseen domain resolved to the destination address two minutes before the connection was established.

**Evidence available:**
- Fri 22:14 — DNS resolution: `cdn-us-east-19.streamcloud.io` → `203.0.113.45` (Proxy/DNS; no prior resolution history for this domain)
- Fri 22:15:08 — `CONN_OUT srv-backup-01:51234 → 203.0.113.45:443 ESTABLISHED` (Firewall)
- Firewall data transfer log (incremental):

  | Time | Volume |
  |---|---|
  | 22:16:42 | 2.3 GB |
  | 22:21:15 | 8.7 GB |
  | 22:34:22 | 18.4 GB |
  | 22:42:18 | 20.6 GB |
  | **Total** | **~50.0 GB** |

- `powershell.exe -EncodedCommand` (Fri 22:12) preceded the connection by 3 minutes and is the most likely execution mechanism for the transfer
- No authorized backup job, scheduled task, or change ticket found for this destination IP or domain
- Source: `admin_backup` credentials; host: `srv-backup-01`

**Signal / Noise call:** Signal. A 50 GB outbound transfer to an IP with no organizational history, initiated via encoded PowerShell, from a backup server, during off-hours, with no authorized change on record — the alternative hypothesis (legitimate backup to an undocumented CDN) is not supported by any available evidence. The SIEM/firewall timestamp discrepancy does not change this conclusion; it changes the transfer pattern (single window vs. multiple waves), which may be relevant to the total volume transferred, but does not affect the signal determination.

## Alert 4 — TKT-04203

**Rule Hypothesis:** Firewall rule added on `srv-backup-01` by `admin_backup`, permitting outbound traffic to any destination on port 443, with no associated change management record.

**Evidence available:**
- Sat 02:28 — `scheduled_task_runner.exe` launched `powershell.exe -EncodedCommand` (EDR)
- Sat 02:30:15 — `FW_RULE_ADD by=admin_backup src=srv-backup-01 dst=any:443 action=allow`
- Sat 02:30:18 — `FW_CONFIG_SAVE by=admin_backup`
- Sat 02:30:22 — `FW_RELOAD success`
- No change ticket or maintenance window found for this modification; the change calendar was last updated two weeks before the incident — **absence of a scheduled window is noted but not conclusive without confirming the calendar was actively maintained**
- Email gateway: no emails to/from `admin_backup` during Fri–Sat window — email-based authorization or instruction is ruled out

**Signal / Noise call:** Signal. A persistent firewall rule opened without a change ticket, during off-hours, using a service account, immediately following the execution of an encoded PowerShell command initiated by a scheduled task — this is consistent with a persistence mechanism, not an administrative oversight. The fact that `admin_backup` had administrative privileges to make this change does not authorize the change; it indicates the account's permissions are being used beyond their intended operational scope.

## Alert 5 — TKT-04287

**Rule Hypothesis:** `admin_backup` added itself to the `Domain_Admins` group via a `net.exe` command executed from `powershell.exe`. The change was replicated across domain controllers.

**Evidence available:**
- Sat 05:58 — `powershell.exe` executed `net.exe localgroup "Domain Admins" admin_backup /ADD` (EDR)
- Sat 06:00:42 — `AD_GROUP_MODIFY user=admin_backup group=Domain_Admins action=ADD executed_by=admin_backup source_host=srv-backup-01`
- Sat 06:00:45 — `AD_REPLICATION_START dc01 → dc02`
- Sat 06:01:12 — `AD_REPLICATION_COMPLETE`
- No evidence of lateral movement or post-escalation activity using the elevated privileges in the available logs — **absence of evidence; lateral movement cannot be ruled out without a wider scope review**
- `admin_backup` is a service account — domain admin group membership is not an expected operational need for a backup service account

**Signal / Noise call:** Signal. A service account adding itself to `Domain_Admins` via PowerShell/net.exe, at 06:00 on a Saturday, at the end of a 15-hour session that included data exfiltration and firewall modification, is not explainable as routine administration. The fact that post-escalation activity is not visible in the current evidence set does not lower the severity — it expands the unknown surface.

## Correlation Summary

The single entity connecting all five alerts is the `admin_backup` account operating from `srv-backup-01`. No alert in isolation is conclusive. As a correlated sequence, they describe a progression from initial access through reconnaissance, exfiltration, persistence, and privilege escalation — the five independently-closed false positives form a single, internally-consistent incident narrative.

| Ticket | Standalone Call | Correlated Call | Operational Severity |
|---|---|---|---|
| TKT-04102 | False Positive | Entry point — session anchor | MEDIUM |
| TKT-04118 | False Positive | Signal — anomalous execution preceding exfiltration | CRITICAL |
| TKT-04156 | False Positive | Signal — primary exfiltration event | CRITICAL |
| TKT-04203 | False Positive | Signal — persistence via unauthorized firewall rule | CRITICAL |
| TKT-04287 | False Positive | Signal — privilege escalation to Domain Admins | CRITICAL |

The original closures were individually defensible; the missed step was correlating shared entities across shift handoffs. This is the investigation gap this case illustrates.

> **One open item:** the SIEM severity rating on TKT-04287 was LOW at the time of closure. A Domain Admins group modification by a service account should trigger a higher baseline rule severity — this is a detection engineering calibration gap, separate from the analytical gap in the original false-positive closure, and worth documenting as a post-incident recommendation.
